Hydration

Collapsible Water Bowl for Cats

How do you give a cat water while hiking? A lightweight collapsible bowl clips to your pack and gives your cat an easy place to drink without taking up much room. Carry water separately, and look for a bowl that's sturdy when open, simple to clean, and appropriately sized for your cat.

Paw Care

Cat-Safe Paw & Coat Wipes

What wipes are safe to use on a cat's paws and coat? For ordinary trail dust or mud, a damp cloth may be all you need. If you prefer wipes, choose a grooming product labeled for cats and follow its directions—never use household disinfecting wipes. They're handy for a quick cleanup after a dusty trail loop.

Grooming

Self-Cleaning Cat Slicker Brush

Is a self-cleaning slicker brush worth it for cats? The release button makes cleanup much easier by pushing collected fur off the pins, so you don't have to pull it out by hand—especially convenient when loose fur bothers your allergies. Look for sturdy pins, a comfortable grip, and a release button that holds up to regular use.

How do you remove burrs, stickers, and goat heads from a cat's coat after a walk? The EasyGroomer lifts trail debris and loose fur without letting everything get buried in a traditional brush, and it's also a useful deshedding tool for longer-haired cats.
